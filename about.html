About

A translational scientist driven by curiosity — and by the people therapies could one day reach.

Molecular biology, biochemistry, and immunology converge in my work to engineer immune cells that are safer, smarter, and more durable than the ones nature gave us.

Research Statement

The story behind the science.

I have a long-standing interest in translational sciences and a deep motivation to bring gene- and cell-based therapies from the bench to the patients who need them most. Trained at the intersection of molecular biology, biochemistry, and immunology, I have devoted my career to understanding the mechanisms that govern immune cell function, regulation, and homeostasis — with the goal of building novel, stable, and potent regulatory T cell (Treg) therapies that can induce immune tolerance in the context of transplantation and autoimmune disease.

My current work brings together chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) design and the engineering of primary human immune cells via CRISPR/Cas9, lentiviral, and adeno-associated virus (AAV)-mediated knock-in approaches. Looking ahead, I am especially excited about leveraging this skillset to develop cell therapies for neurodegenerative diseases — translating mechanistic insight into transformative clinical impact.
