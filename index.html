i. Research focus

From discovery to durable therapy.

I combine molecular biology, genome editing, and translational immunology to design cell therapies that promise true, long-term tolerance — for transplant recipients, patients living with autoimmunity, and one day, for neurodegenerative disease.

01.

CRISPR/Cas9 & AAV-HDR Engineering

High-efficiency genome editing in primary human Tregs — over 95% knockout, ~90% single knock-in, and ~75% dual knock-in under endogenous promoters.

02.

Next-Generation CAR Architectures

Designing tonic-signal-attenuated CARs with IL-2Rβ signaling domains to deliver antigen-triggered, cell-intrinsic support for Tregs in vivo.

03.

Xenotransplantation Tolerance

Engineering swine-leukocyte-antigen (SLA) CAR-Tregs paired with regenerative payloads — PD-L1, Amphiregulin — to suppress rejection and promote repair.

04.

Single-Cell Translational Biology

Integrating CITE-seq with V(D)J profiling on tolerant-liver biopsies to surface clinically actionable biomarkers of operational immune tolerance.

05.

Lineage Stability & Metabolism

Mapping the PI3K–AKT–mTOR signaling axis and oxidative-phosphorylation phenotypes that drive long-lived, suppression-competent Tregs.

06.

Scalable Manufacturing

Optimizing G-Rex gas-permeable expansion for clinically relevant Treg yields with superior metabolic fitness and in vivo function.

Every cell therapy that ever helped a patient began as a hypothesis someone refused to abandon.
