Research Focus

From discovery to durable therapy.

I combine molecular biology, genome editing, and translational immunology to design cell therapies that promise true, long-term tolerance — for transplant recipients, patients living with autoimmunity, and one day, for neurodegenerative disease.

CRISPR/Cas9 & AAV-HDR Engineering

High-efficiency genome editing in primary human Tregs — >95% knockout, ~90% single knock-in, and ~75% dual knock-in under endogenous promoters.

Next-Generation CAR Architectures

Designing tonic-signal-attenuated CARs with IL-2Rβ signaling domains to deliver antigen-triggered, cell-intrinsic support for Tregs in vivo.

Xenotransplantation Tolerance

Building swine-leukocyte-antigen (SLA) CAR-Tregs paired with regenerative payloads (PD-L1, Amphiregulin) to suppress rejection and promote repair.

Single-Cell Translational Biology

Integrating CITE-seq + V(D)J profiling on tolerant-liver biopsies to surface clinically actionable biomarkers of operational immune tolerance.

Lineage Stability & Metabolism

Mapping PI3K–AKT–mTOR signaling and oxidative-phosphorylation phenotypes that drive long-lived, suppression-competent Tregs.

Scalable Manufacturing

Optimizing G-Rex gas-permeable expansion for clinically relevant Treg yields with superior metabolic fitness and in vivo function.

Currently

At Mayo Clinic, Arizona.

Leading and co-leading five active translational programs in the Khosravi-Maharlooei Lab — spanning CRISPR-engineered Tregs, CAR-Treg optimization, IL-2 receptor signaling, single-cell immune profiling of tolerant liver transplants, and scalable Treg manufacturing platforms. Co-investigator on an NIH R01 submission and active inventor on two pending Mayo Clinic patents.
