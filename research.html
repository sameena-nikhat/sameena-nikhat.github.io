01.
Project Lead · CRISPR · CD2-KO

Engineering stable, lymphodepleting-conditioning-resistant Treg therapy.

Transplantation · Autoimmunity

Hypothesis

CD2 is expressed on all T cells, including Tregs. In transplant settings, siplizumab (α-CD2) depletes Tregs alongside effectors, eroding tolerance to the graft. We hypothesized that deleting CD2 from Tregs would make them resistant to siplizumab-containing conditioning regimens — and asked how that loss reshapes their phenotype and function.

Research outcomes

  • Applied CRISPR/Cas9 to achieve over 95% CD2 knockout in primary human Tregs; functionally validated resistance to siplizumab-mediated cytotoxicity.
  • Profiled WT vs KO Tregs with multi-parametric spectral and conventional flow (exhaustion, senescence, mitochondrial fitness), in vitro suppression, Seahorse metabolic profiling, and in vivo GVHD models.
  • Performed bulk RNA-seq and ATAC-seq for gene expression and chromatin accessibility; expressed HLA-A2 CARs (lentiviral) and ran Treg-to-T-helper conversion assays — showing improved lineage stability of CD2-KO Tregs.
  • Mechanism uncovered via phospho-proteomics and western blotting: attenuation of the PI3K–AKT–mTOR axis as a driver of enhanced Treg stability and reduced inflammatory potential.
02.
Project Lead · CAR-Treg · Xenotransplant

Optimized SLA CAR-Treg therapy for immune tolerance in xenotransplantation.

Porcine grafts · CRISPR/AAV

Hypothesis

Given the critical shortage of donor organs, porcine xenografts represent a promising alternative — but robust xenogeneic immune responses remain a major barrier. We hypothesized that engineering SLA-class-I-specific CAR-Tregs would enable targeted suppression of anti-pig responses. To overcome tonic-signaling exhaustion and poor in vivo persistence, we proposed physiologically-tuned CAR architectures, IL-2Rβ signaling integration, and dual immunosuppressive/regenerative payloads (PD-L1, Amphiregulin).

Research outcomes

  • Screened six SLA-specific CAR constructs from hybridoma-derived scFvs in TCR-KO Jurkat reporters; identified SLA #1 CAR as lead based on robust activation (↑CD69, NFκB, NFAT, AP-1) across multiple SLA-I haplotypes.
  • Evaluated in vivo function in NSG MHC-II⁻/⁻ humanized (HIS) mouse model of porcine fetal lung xenotransplant.
  • Engineered next-gen CAR designs (CD3ζ-only, CD28/CD3ζ, CD28/CD3ζ/IL2Rβ, HIT) via CRISPR/AAV-HDR knock-in under endogenous TCRα — achieving ≥90% single KI and ~75% dual KI with PD-L1 / Amphiregulin under endogenous PD-1 control.
  • Functional assays show reduced tonic signaling in HIT designs with preserved antigen specificity, and enhanced suppressive capacity over conventional CARs.
  • Ongoing efficacy work in humanized NSG MHC⁻/⁻ pig-lung and islet-graft models with downstream graft function, immune infiltration, and tissue-repair read-outs.
03.
Project Co-Lead · IL-2Rβ · HLA-A2

Next-generation HLA.A2 CAR-Tregs with IL-2Rβ-chain signaling.

GVHD · Allotransplantation

Hypothesis

CAR-Treg therapy is limited by IL-2 scarcity in vivo — undermining Treg survival, expansion, and FOXP3 lineage stability. Systemic low-dose IL-2 lacks specificity and can expand NK and effector cells. We hypothesized that incorporating an IL-2Rβ signaling domain (with STAT-5 binding motifs) into a CD3ζ+CD28 CAR would enable antigen-triggered, cell-intrinsic IL-2 signaling — delivering next-generation CAR-Tregs with superior persistence.

Research outcomes

  • Engineered primary human Tregs with an HLA.A2 CAR containing IL-2Rβ signaling via CRISPR/AAV-HDR knock-in under endogenous TCRα (~90% integration).
  • Demonstrated enhanced Treg stability and function (↑HELIOS, CD39, CTLA-4) with reduced exhaustion/senescence (↓CD57, H2AX, TOX) vs conventional CD3ζ+CD28 CAR-Tregs.
  • Seahorse profiling showed a shift toward oxidative phosphorylation — consistent with improved Treg fitness.
  • Robust antigen-specific activation on HLA.A2⁺ targets (↑CD69, HLA-DR, Ki67) with selective ↑pSTAT5.
  • Established therapeutic efficacy in two humanized in vivo models: allotransplant (HLA.A2⁺ fetal lung/skin grafts) and GVHD (NSG HLA.A2-Tg mice). Histology, immune profiling, and graft-localized Treg persistence ongoing.
04.
Project Lead · Single-cell · CITE-seq

Functional markers of immune tolerance in liver transplant recipients.

10X Genomics · V(D)J profiling

Hypothesis

Spontaneous operational tolerance — graft function maintained without long-term immunosuppression — represents a unique immunological state whose mechanisms remain poorly understood. We hypothesized that tolerant recipients carry distinct intrahepatic immune signatures (clonal TCRs, regulatory vs pro-inflammatory states) and that integrating single-cell V(D)J + CITE-seq with peripheral immunophenotyping would surface clinically actionable, non-invasive biomarkers.

Research outcomes

  • Built a translational single-cell pipeline for liver-biopsy immune profiling — same-day dissociation, CD45⁺ enrichment, parallel preservation of CD45⁻ stromal fractions.
  • Procured and processed biopsy samples from tolerant and non-tolerant transplant recipients at Mayo Clinic Hospital.
  • Generated single-cell libraries on the 10X Genomics platform integrating CITE-seq antibody barcoding with V(D)J profiling — completed GEM generation, RT, and cDNA amplification for gene-expression, TCR-repertoire, and feature-barcode libraries.
  • Currently staged for pooled library processing and deep sequencing.
05.
Project Co-Lead · Manufacturing

G-Rex vs traditional methods for generating potent Tregs for cell therapy.

Bioprocess · Scale-up

Hypothesis

G-Rex gas-permeable systems offer scalable, efficient immune-cell manufacturing — proven for CAR-T, TILs, and NK — but their suitability for Treg expansion was unexplored. We ran a head-to-head comparison vs traditional flasks across phenotype, metabolic fitness, function, and safety.

Research outcomes

  • Established a 5-week side-by-side expansion platform — G-Rex significantly outperformed flasks in kinetics and viability.
  • Multi-parametric flow showed maintained FOXP3 with enhanced functional/homing markers (↑FOXP3, CTLA-4, CD62L, CCR7) and reduced exhaustion.
  • CFSE suppression assays demonstrated higher inhibitory capacity of G-Rex-expanded Tregs.
  • Seahorse profiling revealed a shift toward oxidative phosphorylation with reduced glycolysis — a metabolically fit, persistent phenotype.
  • In NSG GVHD models, G-Rex-expanded Tregs conferred significantly improved survival, reduced disease severity, and decreased weight loss vs flask-expanded counterparts.

06.
Doctoral · DST-funded · PNAS 2021

microRNAs essential for B vs T cell fate of haematopoietic progenitors.

Research outcomes

Identified microRNAs expressed during differentiation of haematopoietic progenitors into committed B or T lymphocytes, profiled their target genes, and elucidated their functional role during early lymphocyte development. Findings published in Nikhat et al., PNAS (2021).

Technical highlights

  • Isolated and quantified total and microRNA fractions across haematopoietic MPPs, myeloid cells, and B/T lymphocytes; performed genome-wide miRNA-seq.
  • RNA-seq and RT-PCR for differential gene expression analysis.
  • Identified global microRNA targetomes in developing B and T lymphocytes via RIP-seq of Ago2-bound mRNAs.
  • Gain/loss-of-function studies, miRNA sponges, real-time reporter assays, and in vitro differentiation of progenitors into immune lineages.
  • Transcription factor binding and global methylation studies via ChIP-seq.
  • Engineered novel retro/lentiviral vector backbones with bidirectional cassettes for inducible multi-mRNA expression.
  • Developed novel fluorescence-based, flow-compatible reporter assays to measure endogenous miRNA activity in difficult-to-transfect immune cells.
07.
Doctoral · DBT-funded · NAR 2017

Gene regulatory networks orchestrating B and T lymphocyte development.

Research outcomes

Probed the regulatory role of transcription factors EBF1, PAX5, FOXO1, and TCF1 during B/T lymphocyte development. Findings published in Boya et al., Nucleic Acids Research (2017).

Technical highlights

  • In vitro cell differentiation assays and expression analyses (microarrays, RT-PCR) following gain/loss of specific genes.
  • Validated direct and indirect targets of master regulators using ChIP, dual luciferase reporters, and flow-based experiments.
  • shRNA-based and CRISPR/Cas9-mediated depletion of Foxo1 in immune cell lines.
  • Co-investigated 3D chromatin architecture in differentiating B cells using 3C and Hi-C techniques.
